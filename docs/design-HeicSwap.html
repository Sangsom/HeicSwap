HeicSwap — “Warm Darkroom”

HeicSwap · 2026-06-19 · ios-product-team · Input: PRD draft v0.9  ·  Direction 04 — Warm Darkroom

This spec builds the entire design system on the chosen direction: Warm Darkroom — a dark-first, photographic identity with a safelight-amber accent and serif display type. It is the anti-utility look: warm and crafted where every competitor is a cold blue grid.

The design thesis — warmth in the chrome, speed in the flow The personality (serif titles, safelight glow, warm surfaces) lives in the chrome, onboarding, empty states, and the signature “developing” animation. The conversion path itself stays crisp: one screen, one primary action, large legible labels, system-sans body. The darkroom metaphor is the brand; it never slows the task.

6. Critical UX Patterns

Onboarding — 3 screens, no account, no paywall

  1. Value: serif “Convert any photo.” — HEIC, RAW, PNG → JPG, PDF. Warm hero of a photo developing.
  2. Privacy (the wedge): “Developed on your device. Never uploaded.” — shield + safelight.
  3. Permission: “Add photos to start” → triggers add-only Photos access; “Skip” top-right throughout. Auth strategy: defer everything; no login ever.

Paywall — hard at the moment of need, but honest

  • Type: hard gate on Pro features & the daily cap; not shown during onboarding.
  • Layout: the ⛊ on-device trust badge sits at the top (you’re paying for unlimited private conversion, not just more of it) → serif headline → 4 benefit ticks → pricing (Yearly $9.99 default-highlighted “Best value” · Weekly $1.99 · Lifetime $19.99) → amber Continue → Restore · Terms · Privacy.
  • Honesty: no fake countdowns, no pre-checked weekly, explicit “cancel anytime.” This is the brand promise — the thing the incumbents’ 1-star reviews beg for.
  • Persistent trust element: the ⛊ On-device badge is a recurring signal shown on Home, Developing, Results and the paywall — privacy is reinforced exactly where users pay.

Empty states

  • First-time: warm “developing photo” illustration + “Drop in a photo to begin” + Add Photos.
  • No results / all removed: neutral “Your queue is empty.”
  • Error: “Couldn’t convert these 2 files” + Retry, in the interface’s warm voice — never a raw error code.

Settings (grouped)

Defaults (format, quality, strip-metadata default) · Subscription (status, Restore, Manage) · Privacy (statement, “nothing is collected”) · About (rate, contact, Terms).

7. Accessibility Requirements

  • ☑ VoiceOver labels on every control (thumbnails: “Photo 3, HEIC, tap to remove”); hints on non-obvious actions.
  • ☑ All tap targets ≥ 44×44pt (queue ✕ buttons get padded hit areas).
  • ☑ WCAG AA contrast — verified pairs: #F4EBE3/#1A1413 ≈ 13:1 · #2A1F1A/#FBF3EC ≈ 12:1 · #6E5D52/#FBF3EC ≈ 4.6:1 · CTA #23100b/#FF7B54 ≈ 7:1. Never white-on-amber.
  • ☑ Dynamic Type — serif titles via .serif design scale with the system; test to XXL.
  • ☑ Reduced Motion — “develop” reveal → crossfade; no brightness sweep.
  • ☑ Don’t rely on color alone — the free-cap indicator always shows text (“10 left”), not just amber.
  • ☑ Bold Text & Smart Invert respected; verify amber doesn’t invert into illegibility.

8. Platform Considerations

  • Dark mode: the hero. Default follows system; Darkroom (dark) and Warm Paper (light) are both first-class. Offer an in-app override in Settings.
  • iPad: supported — wider two-pane (queue left, options right), drag-and-drop import. The safelight glow scales but stays subtle.
  • Landscape: iPhone is portrait-primary for the converter (simpler, faster); iPad supports both. Nothing critical breaks in landscape — the queue grid reflows.
