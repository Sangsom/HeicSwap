1. Opportunity Summary

The idea

HeicSwap is a complete, ad-free, 100% on-device image converter for iPhone: HEIC / RAW / PNG / WebP ⇄ JPG, plus image→PDF — with one-tap batch, resize-to-target-size, and EXIF/GPS stripping. It enters a proven-paying, beatable utility category and wins not on a novel feature, but on execution: the fastest batch, the widest honest format coverage, one clean screen, no ads, no surprise weekly billing, and tight ASO on every format term the incumbents leave on the table.

Origin & why it was revived

An earlier run killed this on differentiation — “HEIC is Sherlocked by Files + Shortcuts, and conversion is one-shot churn.” Under the Execution Pivot, neither is a kill: native overlap and single-use are normal for paid utilities. What matters is that the category demonstrably pays (multiple freemium apps with 9k–17k reviews) and the search results are mixed and beatable — the top app sits at ~17k reviews, with no mega-brand anywhere on the shelf. This is also the seed of a reusable file/media engine (on-device conversion + one-screen UX) that powers planned siblings (SizeFit, UnzipX).

Indirect competitor — the free native path (the “Sherlock” question, answered honestly)

The real substitute isn’t another app — it’s Apple Files + the Shortcuts “Convert Image” action, which converts HEIC→JPG and even batches via “Select Multiple,” for free. We must respect that. But it has hard limits that a paid app legitimately wins:

What the native path does free HEIC→JPG / PNG · basic batch via a hand-built Shortcut · preserve-metadata toggle · save to Photos/Files.
What it can’t do easily — our wedge RAW (DNG/CR2/NEF/ARW) · WebP · image→multi-page PDF · resize to a target file size (“under 2 MB for this form”) · strip EXIF/GPS · one-tap, zero-setup batch · and being discoverable at all — most users never find the Shortcut.

4. Target Audience

Primary persona — “Blocked Sender” (Strategist’s pick)

An everyday iPhone owner who just hit a wall: a website/employer won’t accept their .HEIC, an Android friend can’t open the photo, or an upload form rejects a 6 MB image. Who: 20–55, non-technical, doesn’t know Shortcuts exists. Pain: “I just need a normal JPG / a smaller file / a PDF, right now.” Current solution: a sketchy free converter full of ads, or a random website they upload private photos to. Willingness to pay: low individually but enormous in aggregate — proven by 17k-review incumbents; will happily pay a near-impulse price to make the annoyance disappear ad-free.

This persona is chosen over “pro/RAW photographer” because it maps to the highest-volume head keywords (image converter, heic to jpg, convert image to pdf, image size) and is exactly who the incumbents serve badly. The pro/RAW user is the secondary persona — they supply the recurring-use revenue that a pure single-use audience wouldn’t.

Jobs to be done

  • “When a site rejects my iPhone photo, I want a normal JPG instantly, so I can finish the upload.”
  • “When a form caps file size, I want to shrink a photo to under 2 MB, without guessing.”
  • “When I apply for something, I want to turn 5 photos into one PDF, so I can attach it.”
  • “When I send to Android/Windows, I want a batch of HEICs as JPGs in one tap, without uploading them anywhere.”

5. Monetization Analysis

Recommended model — Freemium + daily cap, hybrid sub & lifetime

The category’s honest objection (the Critic’s flag) is real: a purely single-use converter churns after one batch and won’t sustain subscription MRR. We neutralize it with a hybrid that doesn’t bet on recurring billing alone:

  • Free tier: up to ~10 conversions/day (JPG/PNG/HEIC), watermark-free, no ads. Casual “send 3 photos” users stay happy and free → reviews + word of mouth.
  • Pro — $9.99/yr (hero/default; matches the cleanest ~9k-review incumbent, ~$0.83/mo) or $1.99/wk (secondary, transparent impulse — cancel anytime): unlimited conversions, unlimited batch, RAW, WebP, multi-page PDF, resize-to-target, EXIF strip.
  • Lifetime unlock — $19.99 one-time: the churn-proof tier, priced ~2× the annual so the annual stays the obvious entry (a $9.99 lifetime would cannibalize the sub entirely). The single-use user who’d cancel a sub pays once and we keep them; it also captures the documented “I refuse to subscribe to a utility” segment.

8. Recommendation

VERDICT — BUILD

This is a clean Execution-Pivot win and a strong portfolio opener. The demand is real and recurring, the shelf has no brand wall, and the ranking incumbents share two exploitable flaws — they’re either cloud-based (a privacy liability) or ad-supported (a UX liability) — while the private/on-device alternatives have no marketing behind them. We can be the first well-executed, well-marketed, on-device converter on the shelf.

The one honest risk is the single-use-churn ceiling on subscription MRR. We don’t pretend it away — we design around it with a lifetime unlock + install-volume model, and we treat any recurring-use revenue (RAW/PDF workflows) as upside. What would flip this to PASS: a sudden iOS feature that surfaces conversion natively to mainstream users, or the head keywords being captured by a true mega-brand. Neither is present today.

If BUILD — key success factors

  1. On-device is the headline, not a footnote. Screenshot #1 and the subtitle must say “private / never uploaded.” It’s the single most ownable wedge against the two leaders.
  2. Free has to feel generous. The daily cap is the funnel and the review engine — too stingy and we become the “one photo free” app we’re beating.
  3. Win the long tail of formats. PDF, WebP, RAW, resize-to-size are both the keyword moat and the recurring-use hook the native path can’t match.
  4. Speed is a feature. Instant launch, instant batch, <15 MB — explicitly counter the 173 MB bloated incumbent.
